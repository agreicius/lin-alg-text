Let \(V=\R^n\text{,}\) and let \(\bolde_i\) be the the \(n\)-tuple whose \(i\)-th entry is 1, and whose every other entry is 0: e.g,

\begin{equation*} \bolde_1=(1,0,\dots,0), \bolde_2=(0,1,0,\dots, 0),\text{ etc. } \end{equation*}

Then the set \(B=\{\bolde_1,\bolde_2,\dots \bolde_n\}\) is a basis for \(\R^n\text{,}\) called the standard basis for \(\R^n\text{.}\)
