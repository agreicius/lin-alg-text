Computing the matrix $A$.

\([T(\boldv)]_{B'}=A[\boldv]_B, \text{ for all \(\boldv\in V\) } \text{.}\)

We now use the boxed condition to compute \(A\) column by column. Let \(\boldc_j\) denote the \(j\)-th column of \(A\text{.}\)

By the column method of matrix multiplication we have

\begin{align*} \boldc_j\amp =\amp A\bolde_j \text{ (\(\bolde_j\) the \(j\)-th element of standard basis) }\\ \amp =\amp A[\boldv_j]_B \text{ (\(\boldv_j\) the \(j\)-th element of basis \(B\)) }\\ \amp =\amp [T\boldv_j]_{B'} \end{align*}

Thus we have a formula for computing the \(j\)-th column of \(A\text{:}\)

\begin{equation*} \boldc_j=[T[v_j]]_{B'} \end{equation*}

The matrix \(A\) we obtain in this fashion is called the matrix for \(T\) relative to the bases \(B\) and \(B'\), and is denoted \(A=[T]_{B}^{B'}\text{.}\)
