Suppose the multiplication

\begin{equation*} \underset{m\times m}{E}\cdot\underset{m\times n}{A}=\underset{m\times n}{A'} \end{equation*}

performs a single row operation on \(A\text{.}\) What must \(E\) be? In what follows let \(\boldr_i\) be the \(i\)-th row of \(A\) for \(1\leq i\leq m\text{.}\) \alert{Row addition.} Suppose \(E\) replaces \(\boldr_i\) of \(A\) with \(\boldr_i+c\boldr_j\text{.}\) Then \(E\) is the identity matrix except for its \(i\)-th row.

\begin{equation*} \underset{r_i+cr_j}{E}=\begin{bmatrix}1\amp 0\amp 0\amp \cdots\amp 0\amp 0\\ \vdots\amp \amp \vdots \amp \amp \vdots \\ \cdots\amp c\amp \cdots\amp 1\amp \cdots\amp 0\\ \vdots\amp \amp \vdots \amp \amp \vdots \\ 0\amp 0\amp \cdots\amp 0\amp 0\amp 1 \end{bmatrix} \end{equation*}
