Theorem 3.1.3.

Let \(V\) be a vector space.

  1. \(0\boldv=\boldzero\) for all \(\boldv\in V\text{.}\)

  2. \(k\boldzero=\boldzero\) for all \(k\in \R\text{.}\)

  3. \((-1)\boldv=-\boldv\) for all \(\boldv\in V\text{.}\)

  4. If \(k\boldu=\boldzero\text{,}\) then \(k=0\) or \(\boldu=\boldzero\text{.}\)
