We prove (a), leaving (b)-(d) as an exercise.

First observe that \(0\boldv=(0+0)\boldv\text{,}\) since \(0=0+0\text{.}\)

By Axiom (e) we have \((0+0)\boldv=0\boldv+0\boldv\text{.}\) Thus \(0\boldv=0\boldv+0\boldv\text{.}\)

Add \(-0\boldv\text{,}\) the additive inverse of \(0\boldv\text{,}\) to both sides of the last equation:

\begin{equation*} -0\boldv+0\boldv=-0\boldv+0\boldv+0\boldv.\tag{\(*\)} \end{equation*}

The left-hand side of \((*)\) is \(\boldzero\text{,}\) by Axiom (d). The right-hand side is \(\boldzero+0\boldv=0\boldv\text{,}\) by Axiom (c). We conclude that \(\boldzero=0\boldv\text{.}\)
