Example.

Compute \(\det(A)\) for

\begin{equation*} A=\begin{bmatrix}2\amp 1\amp 0\amp 3\\ 0\amp 2\amp 1\amp 0\\ 4\amp 2\amp 1\amp 0\\ 0\amp 0\amp 0\amp 2 \end{bmatrix} \end{equation*}

Choose the row/column along which you wish to expand wisely! I pick the third column for its preponderance of 0's and 1's:

\begin{align*} \det(A)\amp =\amp 0M_{13}-M_{23}+M_{33}-0M_{43}\\ \amp =\amp -\begin{vmatrix} 2\amp 1\amp 3\\ 4\amp 2\amp 0\\ 0\amp 0\amp 2 \end{vmatrix} + \begin{vmatrix} 2\amp 1\amp 3\\ 0\amp 2\amp 0\\ 0\amp 0\amp 2 \end{vmatrix}\\ \amp =\amp -2\begin{vmatrix} 2\amp 1\\ 4\amp 2 \end{vmatrix} + 2\begin{vmatrix} 2\amp 1\\ 0\amp 2 \end{vmatrix}\\ \amp =\amp -0+8=8\text{.} \end{align*}

What was I thinking? I should have picked the last row!
