Column method of $AB$.

Given a matrix \(A_{m\times n}\text{,}\) we will think of \(A\) as a sequence of \(n\) column vectors:

\begin{equation*} \begin{bmatrix}a_{11}\amp a_{12}\amp \cdots \amp a_{1n}\\ a_{21}\amp a_{22}\amp \cdots \amp a_{2n}\\ \vdots\amp \vdots \amp \cdots\amp \vdots \\ a_{m1}\amp a_{m2}\amp \cdots\amp a_{mn} \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}\vert \amp \vert \amp \amp \vert\\ \bolda_1\amp \bolda_2\amp \cdots \amp \bolda_n\\ \vert\amp \vert \amp \amp \vert \end{bmatrix} \end{equation*}

Then for any column vector \(\boldb=[b_{i}]\) we have

\begin{equation*} A\boldb=A\cdot\begin{bmatrix}b_1\\b_2\\ \vdots \\b_n \end{bmatrix} = b_1\bolda_1+b_2\bolda_2+\cdots +b_n\bolda_n\text{.} \end{equation*}

Next, we treat any \(n\times r\) matrix \(B_{n\times r}\) as a sequence of column vectors \(B=\begin{bmatrix}\vert \amp \vert \amp \amp \vert \\ \boldb_1\amp \boldb_2\amp \cdots\amp \boldb_r\\ \vert \amp \vert \amp \amp \vert \end{bmatrix}\text{.}\) Then we have

\begin{equation*} AB=A\begin{bmatrix}\vert \amp \vert \amp \amp \vert \\ \boldb_1\amp \boldb_2\amp \cdots\amp \boldb_r\\ \vert \amp \vert \amp \amp \vert \end{bmatrix} =\begin{bmatrix}\vert \amp \vert \amp \amp \vert \\ A\boldb_1\amp A\boldb_2\amp \cdots\amp A\boldb_r\\ \vert \amp \vert \amp \amp \vert \end{bmatrix}\text{.} \end{equation*}
