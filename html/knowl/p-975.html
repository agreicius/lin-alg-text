It is easy to get the various details of the change of basis formula wrong. Here is how I organize things in my mind.

  1. We wish to relate \([T]_{B'}\) and \([T]_B\) with an equation of the form \([T]_{B'}=*[T]_B*\text{,}\) where the asterisks are to be replaced with change of basis matrices or their inverses. Think of the three matrices on the RHS as a sequence of three things done to coordinate vectors, reading from right to left.

  2. \([T]_{B'}\) takes as inputs \(B'\)-coordinates of vectors, and outputs \(B'\)-coordinates. Thus the same should be true for \(*[T]_B*\text{.}\)

  3. Since \([T]_B\) takes as inputs \(B\)-coordinates, we must first convert from \(B'\)-coordinates to \(B\)-coordinates. So we should have \([T]_{B'}=*[T]_B\underset{B'\rightarrow B}{P}\text{.}\)

  4. Since \([T]_B\) outputs \(B\)-coordinates, we need to then convert back to \(B'\)-coordinates. Thus \([T]_{B'}=\underset{B\rightarrow B'}{P}[T]_B\underset{B'\rightarrow B}{P}\text{.}\)

  5. If desired you may replace \(\underset{B\rightarrow B'}{P}\) with \(\left( \underset{B'\rightarrow B}{P}\right)^{-1}\text{.}\)
