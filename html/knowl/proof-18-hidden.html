We assume \(\boldr_i=c\boldr_j\) for some \(i\ne j\) and some scalar \(c\text{.}\)

Using the row method of matrix multiplication, we see that the matrix

\begin{equation*} \tilde{A}=\underset{\boldr_i-cr_j}{E}\ \cdot A \end{equation*}

has a row of zeros in the \(i\)-th row. We conclude that \(\det(\tilde{A})=0\text{.}\)

It follows that \(0=\det(\underset{\boldr_i-cr_j}{E}\ \cdot A)=\det \underset{\boldr_i-cr_j}{E}\det A\text{.}\) Since \(\det \underset{\boldr_i-cr_j}{E}\ne 0\text{,}\) we must have \(\det A=0\text{.}\)

The statement about columns follows from the statement about rows and the fact that \(\det(A^T)=\det(A)\text{.}\)
