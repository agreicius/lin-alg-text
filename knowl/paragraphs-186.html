Some standard bases.

  1. Let \(V=\R^n\text{,}\) and let \(\bolde_i\) be the the \(n\)-tuple whose \(i\)-th entry is 1, and whose every other entry is 0: e.g,

    \begin{equation*} \bolde_1=(1,0,\dots,0), \bolde_2=(0,1,0,\dots, 0),\text{ etc. } \end{equation*}

    Then the set \(B=\{\bolde_1,\bolde_2,\dots \bolde_n\}\) is a basis for \(\R^n\text{,}\) called the standard basis for \(\R^n\text{.}\)

  2. Let \(V=P_n\text{.}\) Then the set \(B=\{1, x, x^2, \dots x^{n}\}\) is a basis for \(V\text{,}\) called the standard basis for \(P_n\text{.}\)

  3. Let \(V=M_{mn}\text{,}\) and let \(E_{ij}\) be the \(m\times n\) matrix whose \(ij\)-th entry is 1, and whose every other entry is 0. Then \(B=\{E_{ij}\colon 1\leq i\leq m, 1\leq j\leq n\}\) is a basis for \(V\text{,}\) called the standard basis for \(M_{mn}\text{.}\)
