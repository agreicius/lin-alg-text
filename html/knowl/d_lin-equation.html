Definition 1.1.1. Linear equations.

A linear equation in the \(n\) unknowns (or variables) \(x_1,x_2,\dots x_n\) is an equation of the form

\begin{equation*} c_1x_1+c_2x_2+\cdots +c_nx_n+d=e_1x_1+e_2x_2+\cdots +e_nx_n+f, \end{equation*}

where \(d, f, c_1,\dots, c_n, d_1,\dots, d_n\) are real numbers. An equation not of this form is called nonlinear.

Any linear equation in the unknowns \(x_1,x_2,\dots, x_n\) can be simplified, using only addition and subtraction, to an equation of the form

\begin{equation} a_1x_1+a_2x_2+\cdots a_nx_n=b,\tag{1.1.1} \end{equation}

which we call its standard form.

Given a linear equation with standard form as in (1.1.1), the equation is called homogeneous if \(b=0\text{,}\) and nonhomogeneous if \(b\ne 0\text{.}\)
