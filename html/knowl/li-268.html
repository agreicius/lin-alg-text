Item 2.

(\alert{Quantitative}) Let \(\lambda_1, \lambda_2, \dots, \lambda_r\) be the distinct eigenvalues of \(T\text{,}\) let \(W_{\lambda_j}\) be the corresponding eigenspaces, and let \(n_j=\dim W_{\lambda_j}\text{.}\) Then \(T \text{ is diagonalizable } \Longleftrightarrow n_1+n_2+\cdots +n_r=n\text{.}\)
