(ii) We must prove the implication \(\boldw_1, \boldw_2\in W\Rightarrow \boldw_1+\boldw_2\in W\text{.}\)

\begin{align*} \boldw_1,\boldw_2\in W\amp \Rightarrow\amp \boldw_1=(t,t), \boldw_2=(s,s) \text{ for some \(t,s\in\R\) }\\ \amp \Rightarrow\amp \boldw_1+\boldw_2=(t+s,t+s)\\ \amp \Rightarrow\amp \boldw_1+\boldw_2\in W\text{.} \end{align*}
