Suppose \(A\) is invertible, and let \(A^{-1}\) be its inverse. The claim above is that \(B=\left(A^{-1}\right)^T\) is the inverse of \(A^T\text{.}\) To prove this claim we need only show that \(BA^T=A^TB=I_n\text{:}\)

\begin{align*} BA^T=\left(A^{-1}\right)^TA^T \amp =\amp \left(AA^{-1}\right)^T \ \text{ (since \((CD)^T=D^TC^T\)) }\\ \amp =\amp I_n^T=I_n \ \checkmark \end{align*}
\begin{align*} A^TB=A^T\left(A^{-1}\right)^T \amp =\amp \left(A^{-1}A\right)^T \ \text{ (since \((CD)^T=D^TC^T\)) }\\ \amp =\amp I_n^T=I_n \ \checkmark \end{align*}
