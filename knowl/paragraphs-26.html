So how do we get ALL solutions, and describe them in a useful way? Here's how

  1. Divide the variables \(x_1, x_2, \dots, x_5\) into leading variables and free variables: a leading variable is one corresponding to a column of the row reduced matrix that contains a leading 1; a free variable is any variable that is not a leading one. In our case, \(x_1, x_3\text{,}\) and \(x_5\) are leading, and the rest, \(x_2, x_4\) are free.

  2. The free variables act as parameters for our solutions. They range independently over all reals. Give them parameter names: \(x_2=s\text{,}\) \(x_4=t\text{.}\)

  3. Solve for the remaining leading variables in terms of the parameters \(s, t\text{,}\) starting from the rightmost leading variable and working backwards ( “backwards substitution”). We find \(x_5=2\text{,}\) \(x_3=-6+7=1\text{,}\) \(x_1=7-2s-3t\text{.}\)

Thus the general solution of the system is \((x_1,x_2,\dots,x_5)=(7-2s-3t, s, 1, t, 2)\text{,}\) where \(s,t\) can be any real numbers.
