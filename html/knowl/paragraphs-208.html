Proof of change of basis theorem.

{ \alert{Theorem recap}. Let \(V\) be finite-dimensional, let \(T\colon V\rightarrow V\) be linear, let \(B\) and \(B'\) be two bases for \(V\text{,}\) and let \(A=[T]_B\text{,}\) \(A'=[T]_{B'}\text{.}\) Then \(A=(\underset{B\rightarrow B'}{P})^{-1}A'\underset{B\rightarrow B'}{P}\) and \(A'=(\underset{B'\rightarrow B}{P})^{-1}A\underset{B'\rightarrow B}{P}\) }

First recall that \(\underset{B'\rightarrow B}{P}=(\underset{B\rightarrow B'}{P})^{-1}\text{.}\) Using this fact, it is easy to see that the second equality follows from the first by multiplying on the left and right by an appropriate matrix and its inverse.

We prove the first equality using the uniqueness property of \(A=[T]_B\text{.}\)

That is, set \(C=(\underset{B\rightarrow B'}{P})^{-1}A'\underset{B\rightarrow B'}{P}\text{.}\)

To show \(C=A\text{,}\) we need only show \([T(\boldv)]_B=C[\boldv]_B\) for all \(\boldv\in V\text{.}\) Here goes:

\begin{align*} C[\boldv]_B\amp =\amp (\underset{B\rightarrow B'}{P})^{-1}A'\underset{B\rightarrow B'}{P}[\boldv]_B\\ \amp =\amp \underset{B'\rightarrow B}{P}A'\underset{B\rightarrow B'}{P}[\boldv]_B\hspace{10pt} (\text{ since } \underset{B'\rightarrow B}{P}=(\underset{B\rightarrow B'}{P})^{-1})\\ \amp =\amp \underset{B'\rightarrow B}{P}A'[\boldv]_{B'} \hspace{10pt} \text{(prop. of \(\underset{B\rightarrow B'}{P}\))}\\ \amp =\amp \underset{B'\rightarrow B}{P}[T(\boldv)]_{B'} \hspace{10pt} \text{(prop. of \(A'=[T]_{B'}\))}\\ \amp =\amp [T(\boldv)]_{B} \end{align*}

Amazing! We didn't even have to get our hands dirty.
