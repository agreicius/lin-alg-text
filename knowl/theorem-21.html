Theorem 2.2.1.

Let \(A\) be \(n\times n\text{.}\) Then:

  1. \(\det(\rho_{c\cdot r_i}(A))=c\det(A)\text{,}\)

  2. \(\det(\rho_{r_i\leftrightarrow r_j}(A))=-\det(A)\text{,}\)

  3. \(\det(\rho_{r_i+cr_j}(A))=\det(A)\text{.}\)

Equivalently, we have:

  1. \(\det(\underset{c\cdot r_i}{E}\cdot A)=c\det(A)\text{,}\)

  2. \(\det(\underset{r_i\leftrightarrow r_j}{E}\cdot A)=-\det(A)\text{,}\)

  3. \(\det(\underset{r_i+cr_j}{E}\cdot A)=\det(A)\text{.}\)
