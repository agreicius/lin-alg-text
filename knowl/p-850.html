Let \(V, W\) be finite-dimensional vector spaces, let \(T\colon V\rightarrow W\) be linear, let \(B=\{\boldv_1,\boldv_2,\dots, \boldv_n\}\) be a basis for \(V\text{,}\) and let \(B'\) be a basis for \(W\text{.}\) There is a unique matrix \(A\) satisfying the following property:

\begin{equation*} [T(\boldv)]_{B'}=A[\boldv]_B \text{ for all \(\boldv\in V\) }\text{.} \end{equation*}
