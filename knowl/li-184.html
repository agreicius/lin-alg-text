Item 1.

Let \(B=\{1,x, x^2\}\) be the standard basis of \(V\text{.}\) Computing \([p(x)]_B\) in this case is easy since any polynomial \(p(x)=a+bx+cx^2\) is already expressed as a linear combination of the elements of \(B\) !! That is, we have \([a+bx+cx^2]_B=(a,b,c)\) for any polynomial \(p(x)=a+bx+cx^2\text{.}\) Some explicit examples:

\begin{align*} _B\amp = (7, -\pi, \sqrt{2})\\ [1+9x^2]_B\amp =(1,0,9) \end{align*}
