Suppose the multiplication

\begin{equation*} \underset{m\times m}{E}\cdot\underset{m\times n}{A}=\underset{m\times n}{A'} \end{equation*}

performs a single row operation on \(A\text{.}\) What must \(E\) be? In what follows let \(\boldr_i\) be the \(i\)-th row of \(A\) for \(1\leq i\leq m\text{.}\) \alert{Scale.} Suppose \(E\) replaces \(\boldr_i\) of \(A\) with \(c\cdot\boldr_i\) for \(c\ne 0\text{.}\) Then \(E\) is the identity matrix except for the \(i\)-th row, where the 1 is replaced with \(c\text{.}\)

\begin{equation*} \underset{cr_i}{E}=\begin{bmatrix}1\amp 0\amp 0\amp \cdots\amp 0\amp 0\\ 0\amp 1\amp 0\amp \cdots\amp 0\\ \vdots\amp \amp \vdots \amp \amp \vdots \\ 0\amp \cdots\amp 0\amp c\amp 0\amp \cdots\\ \vdots\amp \amp \vdots \amp \amp \vdots \\ 0\amp 0\amp \cdots\amp 0\amp 0\amp 1 \end{bmatrix} \end{equation*}
