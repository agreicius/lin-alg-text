Chapter 2. Section 2.3: further properties of the determinant.

Our analysis of how the determinant changes with row operations leads to a number of very important theorems.

Theorem 2.3.1. Determinant is multiplicative.

Let \(A,B\) be \(n\times n\text{.}\) Then

\begin{equation*} \det(AB)=\det(A)\det(B)\text{.} \end{equation*}
Comment:.

please observe, the same is NOT true of addition!

\begin{equation*} \det(A+B)\alert{\ne}\det(A)+\det(B)\text{.} \end{equation*}
