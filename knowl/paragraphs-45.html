Transpose of a matrix.
Definition 1.3.7.

Given an \(m\times n\) matrix \(A=[a_{ij}]\) its transpose \(A^T\) is the matrix whose \(ij\)-entry is the \(ji\)-th entry of \(A\text{.}\) Using notation we have \(A^T=[b_{ij}]_{n\times m}\text{,}\) where \(b_{ij}=a_{ji}\text{.}\) Note in particular that \(A^T\) is \(n\times m\text{.}\)

Examples:.

let \(A=\begin{bmatrix}1\amp 2\amp 3\\4\amp 5\amp 6 \end{bmatrix}\text{;}\) then \(A^T=\begin{bmatrix}1\amp 4\\2\amp 5\\3\amp 6 \end{bmatrix}\text{.}\) Let \(B=\begin{bmatrix}1\\0\\3 \end{bmatrix}\text{,}\) then \(B^T=\begin{bmatrix}1\amp 0\amp 3 \end{bmatrix}\text{.}\)

Comment:.

there are a couple of equivalent ways of describing \(A^T\) that may be more helpful depending on the situation:

  • \(A^T\) is the matrix whose columns are the rows of \(A\text{.}\)

  • \(A^T\) is the matrix whose rows are the columns of \(A\text{.}\)
