Row method of $AB$.

Given a matrix \(B_{n\times r}\text{,}\) we will think of \(B\) as a sequence of \(m\) row vectors:

\begin{equation*} \begin{bmatrix}b_{11}\amp b_{12}\amp \cdots \amp b_{1r}\\ \hline b_{21}\amp b_{22}\amp \cdots \amp b_{2r}\\ \hline \vdots\amp \vdots \amp \cdots\amp \vdots \\ \hline b_{n1}\amp b_{n2}\amp \cdots\amp b_{nr} \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}-\boldb_1-\\ -\boldb_2- \\ \vdots \\ -\boldb_n- \end{bmatrix} \end{equation*}

Then for any row vector \(\bolda=\begin{bmatrix}a_1\amp a_2\amp \cdots \amp a_n \end{bmatrix}\) we have

\begin{equation*} \bolda B=\begin{bmatrix}a_1\amp a_2\amp \cdots \amp a_n \end{bmatrix} B= a_1\boldb_1+a_2\boldb_2+\cdots +a_n\boldb_n\text{.} \end{equation*}

Next, we treat any \(m\times n\) matrix \(A_{m\times n}\) as a sequence of row vectors and compute

\begin{equation*} AB=\begin{bmatrix}-\bolda_1- \\ -\bolda_2- \\ \vdots \\ -\bolda_m- \end{bmatrix} B = \begin{bmatrix}-\bolda_1B- \\ -\bolda_2B- \\ \vdots \\ -\bolda_mB- \end{bmatrix} \end{equation*}
