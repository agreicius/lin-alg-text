Change of basis for transformations.

We now investigate how our choice of basis affects matrix representations of linear transformations. As mentioned above, we only consider the special case where \(T\colon V\rightarrow V\) and we are comparing matrix representations \([T]_B\) and \([T]_{B'}\) for two choices of basis for \(V\text{.}\)

Theorem 4.5.1. Change of basis for transformations.

Let \(V\) be finite-dimensional, let \(T\colon V\rightarrow V\) be linear, and let \(B\) and \(B'\) be two bases for \(V\text{.}\)

Then

\begin{align*} _{B'}\amp =\underset{B\rightarrow B'}{P}[T]_B\underset{B'\rightarrow B}{P}\\ \amp =\left( \underset{B'\rightarrow B}{P}\right)^{-1}[T]_B\underset{B'\rightarrow B}{P} \end{align*}
