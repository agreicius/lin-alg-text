Adjoint formula for $A^{-1}$.

Its worth recording also that we can use the determinant to give an actual formula for computing the inverse of a matrix!

We all love formulas, but keep in mind that it is faster to use our Gaussian elimination algorithm for computing \(A^{-1}\text{.}\)

Theorem 2.3.3. Adjoint formula.

Let \(A\) be \(n\times n\text{,}\) and suppose \(\det(A)\ne 0\text{.}\)

We define the adjoint matrix of \(A\) as

\begin{equation*} \adjoint(A)=\left([C_{ij}]\right)^T; \end{equation*}

that is, the \(ij\)-th entry of the adjoint matrix is the \(ji\)-th cofactor of \(A\text{.}\)

Then

\begin{equation*} A^{-1}=\frac{1}{\det(A)}\adjoint(A)\text{.} \end{equation*}
Proof.
