Specially sized matrices.

An \(n\times n\) matrix is called a square matrix of order \(n\).

\begin{equation*} A=\varmatrix{n}{n}{a}\text{.} \end{equation*}

A \(1\times n\) matrix is called a row vector:

\begin{equation*} \mathbf{a}=\begin{bmatrix}a_1\amp a_2\amp \cdots \amp a_n \end{bmatrix}\text{.} \end{equation*}

An \(m\times 1\) matrix is called a column vector:

\begin{equation*} \mathbf{b}=\begin{bmatrix}b_1\\ b_2\\\vdots \\ b_m \end{bmatrix} \end{equation*}
