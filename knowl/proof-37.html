Proof

Suppose by contradiction that \(P_\infty\) did have a finite basis \(B=\{p_1,p_2,\dots, p_n\}\) for some polynomials \(p_i(x)\text{.}\)

Each polynomial \(p_i\) has a degree \(m_i=\deg(p_i)\text{.}\) Let \(m\) be the maximum of \(m_1,m_2,\dots, m_n\text{.}\) Then \(\Span(S)\subset P_m\text{,}\) the space of polynomials of degree at most \(m\text{.}\)

But then we have

\begin{equation*} \Span(B)\subset P_m\subsetneq P_\infty\text{.} \end{equation*}

Thus \(B\) doesn't span \(P_\infty\text{.}\) A contradiction.
