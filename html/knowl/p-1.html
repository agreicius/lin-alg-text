A linear equation in the \(n\) unknowns (or variables) \(x_1,x_2,\dots x_n\) is an equation of the form

\begin{equation*} c_1x_1+c_2x_2+\cdots +c_nx_n+d=e_1x_1+e_2x_2+\cdots +e_nx_n+f, \end{equation*}

where \(d, f, c_1,\dots, c_n, d_1,\dots, d_n\) are real numbers. An equation not of this form is called nonlinear.
