From our theorem above, it follows that

\begin{align*} \det(B)\amp =\amp \det(E_r)\det(E_{r-1}\cdots E_1A)\\ \amp =\amp \det(E_r)\det(E_{r-1})\det(E_{r-2}\cdots E_1A)\\ \amp =\amp \vdots\\ \amp =\amp \det(E_r)\det(E_r{-1})\cdots \det(E_1)\det(A) \end{align*}

and thus that

\begin{equation*} \det(A)=\frac{\det(B)}{\det(E_r)\det(E_{r-1})\cdots \det(E_1)}\text{.} \end{equation*}
