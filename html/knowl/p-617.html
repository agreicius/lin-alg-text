It is easy to see that in fact \(P_n\) is a subspace of \(P\text{,}\) and \(P\) is a subspace of \(C^\infty(X)\text{.}\) Indeed \(P_n\) and \(P\) both contain the zero polynomial \(f(x)=0=0x^n+0x^{n-1}+\cdots 0x+0\text{,}\) which is the same thing as the zero function \(0_X=\boldzero\text{.}\) Furthermore, sums and scalar multiples of polynomials (resp. of polynomials of at most degree \(n\)) are polynomials (resp. polynomials of at most degree \(n\)).
