Change of basis matrix.
Theorem/Definition.

Given a vector space \(V\) of dimension \(n\text{,}\) and two different bases \(B\) and \(B'\text{,}\) there is a unique \(n\times n\) matrix \(\underset{B\rightarrow B'}{P}\) satisfying the following defining property:

\begin{equation*} \underset{B\rightarrow B'}{P}[\boldv]_B=[\boldv]_{B'}\text{.} \end{equation*}

The matrix \(\underset{B\rightarrow B'}{P}\) is called the change of basis matrix (or transition matrix) from \(B\) to \(B'\).
